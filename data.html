For Researchers

Key Datasets

The datasets I draw on most in my research, with notes on coverage, access, and what each is best suited for. See also my field guide on working with credit bureau data.

Surveys & Panels
Survey · Triennial
Survey of Consumer Finances (SCF)
The gold standard for U.S. household wealth and balance sheet data. Collects detailed information on assets, liabilities, income, and demographics, with deliberate oversampling of wealthy households. Run by the Federal Reserve.
Federal Reserve Board · since 1983 · Public use files available
Access data
Panel · Biennial
Panel Study of Income Dynamics (PSID)
The longest-running longitudinal household survey in the world, tracking families and their descendants since 1968. Invaluable for studying wealth accumulation, intergenerational transmission, and life-cycle dynamics.
University of Michigan · since 1968 · Application required
Access data
Survey · Annual
Survey of Household Economics and Decisionmaking (SHED)
Annual Federal Reserve survey on household financial well-being, covering savings buffers, credit access, retirement preparation, housing, and economic resilience. Useful for tracking financial fragility and sentiment.
Federal Reserve Board · since 2013 · Public use files available
Access data
Survey · Monthly Panel
Survey of Consumer Expectations (SCE)
Monthly rotating panel from the NY Fed measuring consumer expectations about inflation, labor markets, credit access, and household finances. Particularly useful for eliciting beliefs and subjective probabilities. I used the SCE in my COVID-19 overconfidence research.
Federal Reserve Bank of New York · since 2013 · Public use files available
Access data
Panel · Biennial
Health and Retirement Study (HRS)
Longitudinal survey of Americans over 50, covering health, wealth, retirement, cognition, and end-of-life planning. The primary resource for studying retirement savings, drawdown behavior, and late-life financial decisions.
University of Michigan / NIA · since 1992 · Application required
Access data
Administrative Data
Administrative · Annual
Home Mortgage Disclosure Act (HMDA)
Loan-level data on nearly all U.S. mortgage applications — originations, denials, applicant demographics, loan terms, and lender identity. The most comprehensive public source for studying mortgage market access and fair lending.
CFPB / FFIEC · since 1975 · Fully public
Access data
Administrative · Quarterly
Consumer Credit Panel (CCP)
A 5% random sample of U.S. credit bureau records from the New York Fed — covering credit scores, debt balances, delinquencies, and utilization across all major product types. Exceptionally large and high-frequency. See my field guide for practical advice on acquiring and using credit bureau data.
Federal Reserve Bank of New York · Equifax · Access via data vendors
About the CCP
Administrative · Ongoing
CFPB Consumer Complaint Database
Public database of consumer complaints submitted to the CFPB across financial products and institutions, with company responses and resolution outcomes. A rich source for studying consumer protection, firm behavior, and financial redress — the subject of my ongoing work with Charlotte Haendler.
Consumer Financial Protection Bureau · since 2011 · Fully public
